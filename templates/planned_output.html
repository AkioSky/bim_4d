Planned Outputs

Planned outputs from the use of the 4D BIM software application:

Management

Data Management

  • collaborative production of an organisations archive to enable a method of continuous improvement
  • integrated project delivery methodology
  • integrated process of data collection for facility management (FM) purposes
  • systematic project delivery methods
  • project change management strategies
  • information consistency
  • key performance indicators (KPI)s

Resource Management

  • integrated 4D BIM process simulation model (used to address resource-constrained scheduling problems)
  • production of strategic alternatives
  • optimised resource allocation
  • energy efficiency
  • lean production
  • reliable prediction of productivity dynamics
  • tracking resources in computer vision (referring to locating moving objects in 2D or 3D across time)

Risk Management

  • collaborative environment
  • construction alternatives
  • identify, analyse, evaluate and respond in real-time
  • cognitive assessment of the potential impact of risks
  • in-depth evaluation of risks with high probability
  • training site workers to identify on- site safety risks
  • simulating on-site potential hazards


Technical

Collaboration

  • multi-discipline collaboration network
  • early identification of potential cause for mitigation
  • visualised construction plans and processes
  • progress monitoring, quality control, operation analysis
  • just in time and lean principles
  • multi- discipline alignment of work tasks
  • real-time warning of construction site safety issues
  • synchronised coordination of sub-contractors

Quality assurance

  • identify potential quality risks
  • identify potential disputes
  • tracking and managing tasks
  • reduced material waste
  • greater design flexibility
  • identify quality deviations
  • development of containment strategies

Visualisation

  • competitive tendering capabilities
  • data rich views
  • dynamic visualisation
  • predict potential safety hazards
  • reduced waste
  • secure investment
  • lean construction management


Value

Asset Management

  • development of asset information model (AIM)
  • intuitive life cycle management strategy
  • production of multiple operation & maintenance (O & M) plans to offset potential environmental and cultural restrictions
  • proactive planning approach based on potential changes to the surrounding landscape
  • reduced operating costs and efficient use of the asset
  • safety management e.g. emergency services access
  • intuitive cognitive data updates

Cost control

  • cognitive design validation
  • consistent and accurate data collection
  • dynamic development of risk assessment
  • tracking and managing changes
  • dynamic production of quantity data
  • production of alternative construction designs in real-time
  • dynamic up-dates of planned vs actual construction progress

Earned value

  • end user satisfaction
  • O & M control in real-time
  • assessment of actual vs planned WIP reports to produce KPIs
  • continuous cognitive development of productivity requirements
  • evaluation methods to assess 4D BIM performance i.e. organisation and management, data collection and analysis
  • production of technical strategies to facilitate efficient and productive use of resources
  • continuity of information flow
